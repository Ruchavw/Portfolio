Project Butterfly

Interesting Statement

A butterfly is a beautiful, day-flying insect known for its colorful, scaly wings, belonging to the order Lepidoptera, related to moths, and undergoing a four-stage life cycle (egg, caterpillar, chrysalis, adult) with complete metamorphosis.

Project Monarch

Role: Researcher

Description: The monarch butterfly or simply monarch is a milkweed butterfly in the family Nymphalidae. Other common names, depending on region, include milkweed, common tiger, wanderer, and black-veined brown.

Project Morpho

Role: Researcher

Description: The morpho butterflies comprise many species of Neotropical butterfly under the genus Morpho. This genus includes more than 29 accepted species and 147 accepted subspecies, found mostly in South America, Mexico, and Central America.

Project Small tortoiseshell

Role: Researcher

Description: The small tortoiseshell is a colourful Eurasian butterfly in the family Nymphalidae. Adults feed on nectar and may hibernate over winter; in warmer climates they may have two broods in a season. While the dorsal surface of the wings is vividly marked, the ventral surface is drab, providing camouflage.

Project Swallowtail

Role: Researcher
